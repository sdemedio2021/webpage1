Hedy Lamar

Actress and Inventor

Facts

  • She was born on November 9, 1914

  • She died on January 19, 2000

  • She was married and divorced six times

  • She was a citizen of Austria and the United States

Biography

Hedy Lamarr was born in 1914 in Vienna, Austria. Her name at the time was Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler. In the late 1920's she was discovered as an actress and moved to Berlin. She later moved back to Vienna and pursued an acting career in many stage roles. In 1937, she went to Paris and met Louis B Mayer, who was scouting talent in Europe. He covinced her to change her name to Hedy Lamar and then brought her to Hollywood in 1938. She was known as "The Worlds Most Beautiful Woman."

In her spare time, Hedy worked on different hobbies and inventions. Some of these inventions included an improved traffic light and a tablet that when put in water, dissovles and makes the drink carbonated. Not many people knew about her inventions at the time. During World War II, she learned that radio controlled torpedos could be easily jammed, which caused the torpedo to go off course. She thought of creating a frequency-hopping signal that could not be tracked or jammed. She was granted a patent for her invention in 1942

Accomplishments

  • Developed a new communications system for World War II
